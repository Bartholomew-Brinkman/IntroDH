    Section Course Description

    Welcome to Introduction to Digital Humanities! This course will help you gain a fundamental understanding of the digital humanities (DH), focusing on both the “digital” and the “humanities.” You can expect to learn concepts, methods, and tools for working with data such as literary texts, historical maps, and visual art while reflecting on what it means to do so as a humanist. We will frequently discuss how the skills you acquire in this course can assist you in future courses as well as help prepare you for a variety of careers after graduation. Particular emphasis will be placed on critically understanding artificial intelligence.
    🔗
    🔗

    Section Course Goals, Learning Objectives, and Competencies

    The following are the main goals for the course, along with learning outcomes and career competencies you can expect to develop. Please note that while these are the goals and competencies that we will most explicitly focus on—and associated learning outcomes are included in the course schedule—we will certainly touch on others. Goals and competencies will be addressed through both formal assignments and less formal exercises.
    🔗
    🔗

    Section Course Texts and Additional Resources

    **Required Text:** Ahmed Bouchentouf: *The Prompt Recipe: A Practical Guide to Prompt Engineering and AI Interaction* (breadbooks, 2025)
    🔗
    **Optional Text:** Johanna Drucker, *The Digital Humanities Coursebook: An Introduction to Digital Methods for Research and Scholarship* (Routledge, 2021). I have scanned relevant chapters and have made these available through Canvas, but you may choose to purchase a hard copy if you prefer.
    🔗
    **Additional course materials:** These include online readings, digital tools, and DH projects, which are all freely available online. Links are provided in the syllabus and on our course Canvas site.

    Section Assignments and Grade Breakdown

    Assignment Semester Percentage
    AI Prompt Engineering 10%
    StoryMap Essay 10%
    Midterm Exam 10%
    Final Exam 10%
    Quizzes 10% (10 x 1% each)
    Responses 30% (15 x 2% each)
    In-Class Attendance and Participation 20%
    *Prompt Engineering*: You will have an opportunity to practice AI prompt engineering in depth as you create a text-based product (e.g. a lesson plan or a podcast script).
    🔗
    *StoryMap Essay*: Near the end of the semester, you will have an opportunity to create an online multimedia essay using ArcGIS StoryMaps.
    🔗
    *Midterm Exam*: Near the midpoint of the semester, you will have an opportunity to demonstrate your knowledge of digital humanities topics as you take an in-class midterm exam.
    🔗
    *Final Exam*: At the end of the semester, you will have an opportunity to demonstrate your comprehensive knowledge of digital humanities topics, as you take a final exam.
    🔗
    *Quizzes*: You will be responsible for multiple Canvas quizzes over the course of the semester. These quizzes are designed to help you identify core concepts from the assigned reading that will allow you to effectively participate in class discussion and activities. You will have an opportunity to drop some of your lowest scores (or choose to not take some quizzes).
    🔗
    *Responses*: You will have many opportunities to submit Canvas responses over the course of the semester. These responses will be low-stakes opportunities to try out a variety of DH tools and methods, and to reflect on these methods. These responses will often be started in class, arising from and providing a basis for further class discussion. You will have an opportunity to drop some of your lowest scores (or choose to not complete all responses).
    🔗
    *Attendance and Class Participation***:** This portion of your grade is designed to help ensure regular and timely attendance, as well as full participation in class conversation and activities, while acknowledging occasional extenuating circumstances. Attendance and participation are worth 20% of your final grade and you will receive an attendance and participation grade at both the midpoint and conclusion of the semester.
    🔗

    Section Assignment Submissions

    Because this is a cumulative course in which content and skills build off of what we have previously learned, it is important for your own intellectual progress and for that of your classmates that you submit all assignments (including quizzes and responses) on time and for these same reasons, I am dedicated to providing timely feedback. In particular, please note the following:
    🔗
    · **Quizzes** are designed to help you check your basic understanding of concepts in preparation for further discussion and practice and are therefore due at the beginning of class on the day for which reading was assigned. Quiz due dates are noted in the syllabus and should be completed through Canvas. Quizzes cannot be made up you may retake them as many times as you would like until they are due and a limited number of your lowest quiz scores will be dropped.
    🔗
    · **Responses** give you opportunities to reflect on class discussions and to formalize work done in class. Response due dates are noted in the syllabus. Since there may be additional questions regarding these responses, there is a one-week grace period for submitting these responses, but I strongly encourage you to not treat this as the de-facto due date so that you are sure to submit everything on time. A limited number of your lowest response scores will be dropped.
    🔗
    · **Formal Assignments** are due on the date indicated on the syllabus. These assignments will lose 10% of their final grade for each day (not class day) they are late unless prior arrangements have been made for exceptional circumstances.
    🔗
    · **Exams** will be completed in our classroom during class or the final exam period unless other accommodations have been made.
    🔗
    🔗

    Section Class Preparation

    The general rule at FSU for time spent preparing for class is a **minimum of two hours of preparation time for every class hour**; thus, you should spend a minimum of eight hours a week preparing for this class. During weeks when you have a major assignment due, you may need more time. You can effectively participate by doing the reading (annotating your text as you read with your comments, questions, and reactions), listening to lectures, taking quizzes, working in class, and completing responses. You should feel free to contact me about any difficulties you may have keeping up with the course material.
    🔗
    🔗

    Section Laptop, Cell Phone, Headphone, and Other Device Usage

    In accordance with FSU policy, you should have a laptop available to complete in-class and out-of-class activities. An advanced tablet may be sufficient but less ideal. If you do not currently have access to a laptop, please let me know and I will help you procure one.
    🔗
    * **Cell Phones:** Cell phones are not acceptable for classwork and are incredibly distracting for me as an instructor and for others in the classroom. They are therefore prohibited entirely from classroom use—this includes when we are working individually or in small groups. If you need to use your cell phone for some reason, please leave the classroom entirely—I will assume that whatever you need to do on your phone is more pressing than what we are doing in class (for example, you are expecting an important text from a family member). I will remind you of this fact as necessary.
    🔗
    * **Laptops/Tablets**: Since laptops and other devices can be used for both academic and other activities (e.g. watching unrelated videos), please be sure to use them for our class activities while in class so that you do miss important information that may not be easily repeated.
    🔗
    * **Headphones**: Please do not use headphones while we are in classroom discussion or small-group work, but there may be some limited opportunity to do this while doing individual work. If you need an accommodation beyond this, please let me know.

    Section Academic Honesty

    Your work must comply with the College Policy Regarding Academic Honesty stated in the College Catalogue. Plagiarism, the use of someone else’s words or ideas as if they were your own, is a serious offense. Consult the Writing Guide at CASA for definitions of plagiarism and examples of correct ways to document summaries, paraphrases, or quotations from the work of others. The penalties for plagiarism vary according to the extent of the offense. *A completely plagiarized essay will result in an F for the whole course.* I will report plagiarism to the Dean of Students, who maintains a database of students who have plagiarized essays or cheated on examinations. I hope that no one in this class will even consider plagiarizing, but speak with me about any problems you may have about achieving the results you hope to get from this course.

    Section Office Hours

    One-on-one discussion during office hours is one of the most important, and generally underutilized, resources available to students. It allows you to develop a rapport with your instructor, where you can receive clarification and elaboration on ideas presented in class, where you can better understand their expectations for you and for the course, and—just as importantly—where you can allow them to get to know you on a more personal level. This can be invaluable down the road when you need academic or career advice or a letter of recommendation. I encourage you to visit my office hours, where we can discuss your general class performance along with any particular questions you may have.

    Section Artificial Intelligence Use

    As generative and agentic AI become increasingly common (already embedded in many research and communication tools we have come to rely on), you will be expected to be proficient in these technologies during your time at FSU and in your future careers. To help you gain this proficiency, you will have multiple opportunities to learn about and with AI in this course. This learning involves not only delivering AI-assisted products, but also demonstrating intellectual processes and developing habits of mind that will allow you to most effectively and critically work with AI (e.g. editing AI-generated text for accuracy, and being aware of biases and resource use). You are therefore encouraged to use AI in this course as appropriate, but need to be transparent in how you do so, acknowledging how and when you use it (specific assignments will often have further citation details). Keep in mind that while AI is increasingly less prone to factual inaccuracies, biases, and “hallucinations,” these do still happen and as the final editor of your work, you are ultimately responsible for what you submit. Additionally, just as I see value in students using AI, I will also use it in my teaching (e.g. developing lesson plans and assignments, and streamlining grading) and assume the same ultimate responsibility for my own AI use. If you have any questions about any of this, please feel free to come speak with me. Please also be aware that these are the policies surrounding AI usage for this particular course. Other courses may have different policies and you should consult the stated policies for that course or ask for clarification from your instructor as needed.

    Section Policy Exceptions

    I understand that things come up. You may unexpectedly be absent for a week due to an illness or family emergency. You may know that you will perpetually be late for a particular class day (your previous class is across campus, etc.). Some of you may be uncomfortable speaking in front of others, but don’t feel that this is an indication of your preparedness and would like to find ways to participate. You may need special assistance to facilitate your learning experience. Please come speak to me. I am happy to work with you and to best facilitate your learning, but will best be able to do so if we can come to an agreement at the outset of the course, or as soon as an issue arises, rather than dealing with things after the fact. By the same token, should I need to significantly modify anything in this syllabus or course schedule, I will be certain to give you as much notice as possible.
